QRAK

The new standard of social networking

Abstract

This is the first branding project I ever did. I was tasked with constructing a pitch deck and designing a mobile application as a project for my Gateway to Business Class. The first deliverable we had was an initial presentation to be critiqued by our peers. While we didn't actually program an application, we designed the UI and UX for what our product would look like if it were to be coded. This project features the two branding phases of the same idea.


QRAK is an iOS application that seeks to revolutionize social-networking by qualifying the relationships we have with the people we interact with.

Phase 1


The app is all about sharing cool, ephemeral content, so I wanted something that would be intriguing and every-changing. Smoke was the initial plan, but I found it difficult to replicate it in an intriguing video format. I chose drops of ink in liquid instead, and bumped the contrast and speed to make it an intense and fleeting experience. All of the smoke in the background of the slides was the original speed of the ink, and slowly morphed in the background of the presentation. The logo was made using the ATLAS font to pay homage to the seemingly fleeting and fun decade of the 1920s and it's art-deco characteristics.

Phase 2


The second phase of the project represents a radical redesign of the initial phase. The look is much cleaner but still features the signature grid. I changed the background to a mountain scene so in our final pitch we could sell the initial launch for Colorado. Legibility was an issue on the slide deck of the previous pitch, so I amped-up the contrast between the text and background by selecting a stronger thicker font, bolding important words, and darkening the background as the slides advanced. Be sure to check-out the final visual presentation of the app. I taught myself After Effects animation over the course of two days to produce the final video.
